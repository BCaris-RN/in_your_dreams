# Scientific Sources

## Solubility

- Warner, M. J., and Weiss, R. F. (1985), *Solubilities of
  chlorofluorocarbons 11 and 12 in water and seawater*, Deep-Sea Research,
  32(12), 1485-1497. https://doi.org/10.1016/0198-0149(85)90099-8
- IAEA (2006), *Use of Chlorofluorocarbons in Hydrology: A Guidebook*,
  Table 3.1 reproduces the Warner-Weiss coefficient sets and units.
  https://www-pub.iaea.org/MTCD/Publications/PDF/Pub1238_web.pdf
- Sander (2023), Henry's law database. The project uses the IUPAC
  solubility-form convention `Hcp = aqueous concentration / partial pressure`.
  https://www.henrys-law.org/henry/

## Model scope

This project is a transparent global representative-box attribution model.
It does not claim to replace spatially resolved ocean circulation, wind,
mixed-layer-depth, sea-ice, or tracer-inventory products.

The interpretation of a warming-driven flux shift is consistent with:

- Wang et al. (2021), *On the effects of the ocean on atmospheric CFC-11
  lifetimes and emissions*, PNAS 118(12). The study finds the global ocean
  remains a CFC-11 sink during the present period and projects a net source
  transition later this century. https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2021528118
